Managing Accounts

Registering as a Coach

A free account lets you save drills to the cloud and access them from any device. Registration takes under a minute.

1

Go to the Home Page

Navigate to the main Lab page. You will see a sign-in form near the top of the page.

2

Switch to "Create Account"

Click the Create account link below the sign-in form. The form expands to show three fields: Display name, Email, and Password.

3

Fill in Your Details

Enter a display name (shown on your saved drills), your email address, and a password of at least 6 characters.

4

Confirm Your Email

Click Sign up →. A confirmation email will be sent — click the link inside it to activate your account.

Tip: Check your spam folder if the email does not arrive within a couple of minutes.
5

Sign In

Once confirmed, return to the home page, click Already have an account?, enter your email and password, then click Sign in →. A "Welcome back" greeting confirms you are authenticated.

You can sign out at any time by clicking the Sign out link next to your name.

Registering a Team

A registered team means you can save practices and share them with other coaches.

1

Go to the Home page

Navigate to the main Lab page (index.html). Next to the coach's sign in, you will see a Manage Account link.

2

Add Team Or Create Team

Click the Add Team button. If you have a team name and pin, click Join a Team. Otherwise, click Create Team. Make sure to save the pin for later. There is no pin recovery option.

Deleting Your Account

If for some reason you want to delete your account, you can do so from the Manage Account page. Note: your practices and teams will be deleted or disbanded, but your drills will remain unless you explictly delete them first

1

Go to the Home Page

Navigate to the main Lab page. Sign into your account and click the Manage Account link.

2

Delete Your Account

Scroll to the bottom of the pop-op under Danger Zone and click the Delete my acount button. You will be asked to confirm before permanent deletion.

Creating, Saving & Deleting Drills

Creating a New Drill

1

Open the Canvas

From the home page click Drill Labs Canvas. A blank rink loads ready for drawing.

2

Name Your Drill

Click the Untitled Drill… text field in the top bar and type a name (e.g. "2-on-1 Rush"). This name appears in your Library and Practice Planner.

3

Choose Your Rink View

Click ◑ Half Rink in the right toolbar to toggle between full-ice and half-ice. Select whichever suits the drill.

4

Draw the Drill

The right toolbar has individual tool buttons plus two flyout palette groups:

Lines & Pen — click or hover the group button to reveal the Line, Arrow, and Pen (freehand) tools.

Equipment — reveals the Pylon (▲), Net (🥅), and Puck (⚫) tools. Click the puck tool then click anywhere on the rink to place a puck.

See Keyboard Shortcuts and Players for further detail.

5

Add a Description & Tags

Scroll below the canvas and type coaching points, key cues, and variations into the description field.

Add tags in the tags bar (e.g. breakout; forecheck; 2-on-1), separated by semicolons. Tags allow searching and filtering drills later.

Saving a Drill

1

Save to the Cloud

Click 💾 Save in the top bar, or press Ctrl+S (Cmd+S on Mac). The drill is saved to your account and appears in the Library.

2

Download a Local Backup

Click Save JSON (or press Alt+S) to download a .json backup to your computer — useful for sharing or offline storage.

3

Load a JSON File

Click Load JSON to open a previously exported .json file. The drill loads onto the canvas ready for editing.

Browsing & Deleting Drills

1

Open the Library

Click 📂 Library in the top bar to see all saved drills. Click any drill name to load it onto the canvas.

2

Delete a Drill

In the Library, find the drill you want to remove and click its Delete button. You will be asked to confirm before permanent deletion.

3

Clear the Current Canvas

Click ✕ Clear Canvas in the toolbar to wipe the canvas and start fresh. The saved cloud copy is not affected — only the drawing on screen is cleared.

Players — Subscripts, Colours & Circles

Select the 🏒 Player tool and click anywhere on the rink to place a player. Switch back to the Select (V) tool and click any player to edit its properties in the right panel.

Placing & Choosing Player Type

1

Set the Type Before Placing

In the right toolbar, use the Player type dropdown to choose the label shown on the icon: F (Forward), D (Defenceman), G (Goalie), C, W, X, or O.

2

Change Type After Placing

Select an existing player with the Select (V) tool. In the Selected properties panel, change the Player dropdown to the desired type.

3

Adjust Player Size

Use the Player size dropdown (Small / Medium / Large / X-Large) before placing to control how large the icon appears on the rink.

Adding Subscripts (e.g. F₁, F₂, D₁)

1

Select the Player

Click the player with the Select (V) tool. The Selected panel will appear in the right toolbar.

2

Enter the Subscript

Find the Sub field at the bottom of the Selected panel. Type a number or short label (up to 2 characters), such as 1 or 2. The icon updates immediately — a Forward labelled 1 becomes F₁.

Tip: Use subscripts to differentiate same-type players — e.g. F₁ starts with the puck while F₂ cuts to the net.

Changing Player Colour

1

Select the Player

Click the player with the Select (V) tool.

2

Change the Stroke Colour

In the Selected panel, click the Stroke colour swatch and pick a colour from the picker. This changes the outline and label colour of the icon.

3

Set a Default Colour

You can also set the stroke colour before placing a player using the Stroke swatch in the main toolbar. All new players will use that colour until changed.

Putting a Circle Around a Player

1

Select the Player

Click the player with the Select (V) tool.

2

Enable the Circle

In the Selected panel, find the Circle checkbox and tick it. A circle will appear around the player icon — useful for marking a coach, a key player, or a starting position.

Other Properties

1

Opacity

Use the Opacity slider in the Selected panel to make any element semi-transparent — handy for optional player positions or ghosted paths.

2

Line Style

For lines and arrows, use the Line style dropdown to choose Solid, Dashed, Dotted, or Wiggle. By convention, solid lines indicate puck movement and dashed lines indicate skating without the puck.

3

Fill (Shapes)

For rectangles and ellipses, tick the Fill checkbox in the Selected panel and choose a fill colour to shade the inside of the shape.

Creating a Practice & Tracking Time

The Practice Lab lets you assemble saved drills into a timed practice plan. Open it by clicking PRACTICE in the canvas top bar, or navigate directly to practice.html.

Setting Up the Practice

1

Name the Practice

Click the Untitled Practice… field at the top and type a name (e.g. "Tuesday U16 — Breakout Emphasis").

2

Set Date & Team

Fill in the Date and Team fields in the metadata row. These appear on the exported PDF.

3

Set Your Ice Slot Duration

Update the target duration field (default is 60 minutes). The timeline bar and remaining-time counter will recalibrate automatically.

Adding Drills

1

Search Your Library

Use the search field in the left panel to find saved drills by name or tag. Click Add next to any drill to append it to your practice plan.

2

Add a Quick Drill

For activities without a diagram (warm-up, stretching, team talk), click Quick Drill, enter a name and description — no canvas required.

3

Reorder Drills

Drag any drill card up or down in the list to reorder the sequence. Time totals update instantly.

Tracking & Checking Remaining Time

1

Set Each Drill's Duration

Each drill card has a minutes input on its right side. Click it and type the number of minutes to allocate. All totals update automatically.

2

Read the Timeline Bar

The timeline bar at the bottom of the practice list fills as you add time. The label to its right shows how many minutes remain in your ice slot.

If total time exceeds your slot, the bar turns orange as a warning and the remaining value goes negative.

3

Check the Running Total

Next to the target duration field, the total counter (e.g. 55:00) shows the sum of all drill durations in mm:ss. It turns red when you exceed the target.

4

Remove a Drill

Click the ✕ button on any drill card to remove it from the practice. The timeline recalculates immediately.

Saving & Exporting

1

Save the Practice

Click 💾 Save to save the practice to your account. Reload it later with the Load button.

2

Export to PDF

Click PDF ↓ to generate a printable PDF — including drill diagrams, descriptions, and timing — ready to hand out on the bench.

3

Share with Other Coaches

Click Share to generate a shareable link you can send to assistant coaches so they can view the practice plan on their own device.
